Ditto Farming Guide

The Basics

Dittos have the most stable and a high base price since they can be bred with all mons. This makes them highly desirable, and any high IVs you find can be sold for additional profit. However, your main income comes from selling "shittos"—Dittos with bad IVs—as whole boxes of 60 to other players who use them for egging shinies.

Usually, the price for a box is around 270k-310k depending on the market, but it's especially lucrative during a shiny-war, where prices can reach up to 350k per box. This guide will showcase 3 ditto catching methods with different entry costs, strategies, and ease of use.

Requirements

• Hoenn or Johto story completed.

• A specialized catching mon from a strategy.

• Catching Streak: This is catching multiple Pokémon of the same species (Ditto).

• Building Streak: Usually built using Dusk Balls.

• Bonus: Maxes at 15; gives a huge bonus to the ball most used (Repeat Ball).

• Warning: Catching another species or inactivity for a few days breaks the streak.

Location

Hoenn: Desert Underpass

Route 114, accessable through the Fossil Maniac's House

Pros: Higher encounter rate; Long tunnel means less inputs

Cons: "Bad" Phases (Whismur line)

Johto: Route 47

Patch of grass via first waterfall when coming from Cianwood and going down the ladder in the first cave

Pros: Legendary bird chance; better phases

Cons: Lower encounter rate; more key inputs

Smeargle Strat

Having False Swipe and Spore only Smeargle's makes this strat brain dead easy and only pp consumtion will be a problem as Smeargle cannot be put to sleep or die.

Consider filling up your party with these cheap Smeargle, so you have to go less often to the Pokecenter to fill up PP.

The held item Safety Goggles disables the effect of spore type moves on smeargle meaning he cant be put to sleep and False Swipe as the only attack ensures smeargle cannot be killed by the wild ditto

Turn order :

1. False swipe

2. Spore

3. catch

4. if it breaks out ball again if it wakes up spore again

Pros :

  • • Low entry cost (Safety goggles ~50k, smeargle as low as 5k)
  • • Low level requirement (level 80 smeargle can put ditto to 1hp)
  • • braindead easy (no breeding only putting moves on smeargle)

Cons :

  • • Most basic version of the strat so no additional benefits other than catching

Snorlax strat

This strat utilizes one of Johto's Ball-maker balls : Heavy Balls ( the heavier the target the higher the catchrate multiplier)

With this ball we can achieve a close to 100% catchrate and rarely ditto breaks out.

Using payday means the snorlax needs exact stats the ditto copies so it lives payday on almost every HP IV and attack roll (These are the stats for Desert Underpass farming):

Exact stats @lvl 100

ATK = 260

DEF = 148

can play around with a bit higher atk and defense but these work for me

Leftovers provide infinite sustain to snorlax

Execution :

Turn 1: Yawn

Turn 2: Payday

Turn 3: Catch

Pros:

  • • 100% catchrate means this strat is the fastest to catch dittos
  • • can utilze payday to offset ball cost
  • • Medium entry cost (leftovers usually <25k, snorlax breed cost low)
  • • Can farm the balls to make catching even cheaper

Cons:

  • • Snorlax needs exact stats meaning annoying EV and level training
  • • Heavy balls can be expensive at times and only buyable from GTL
  • • Even with the cost offset heavy balls are ~1.8x expensive as repeat balls

Espeon strat

The goal of this strat is using Espeons unqiue combintion of moves, ability and stats to catch high value dittos more frequently.

Using Synchronize with Adamant nature gives increased chance (~20%) to get Adamant Dittos which are far more valuable when they have one or more 31 IVs in ATK and/or SPD.

Payday offsets ball cost aswell making this the cheapest way to catch dittos long term. Using payday means the Espeon needs exact stats the Ditto copies so it lives payday on almost every HP IV and attack roll.

Exact stats @lvl 100

ATK = 251

DEF = 125

Depending on the Region you will need a different held item: Muscle Band for Johto and Silk Scarf for Hoenn.

Turn order:

Turn 1: Yawn

Turn 2: Payday

Turn 3: Catch

Turn 4: If Ditto breaks out throw ball again if it wakes up yawn again

Turn 5 (if yawned again): Use non damage move that is not yawn

Pros:

  • • High chance to catch very valueable Dittos
  • • Payday offsets ball cost

Cons:

  • • High entry cost (3 Exact stats breed and different items)
  • • Espeon can die from getting hit too often (might need multiple Espeons or use of revives)
